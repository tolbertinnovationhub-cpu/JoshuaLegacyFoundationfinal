WHO WE ARE

The Joshua Legacy Foundation (TJLF) is an innovative and transformative youth and leadership development, restoration, advocacy and empowerment institution. Incorporated and registered in Liberia in 2020 as a Non-Governmental Organization (NGO), we are committed to serving the holistic and sustainable education needs of Liberia's youth population, with specific emphasis on at-risk street children, orphaned, underprivileged, and vulnerable youth.

We provide structured, purpose-driven interventions designed to empower young people to acquire the mindset and skills needed to assume future leadership roles within their communities and become empowered, wholesome functioning citizens actively involved in the transformation of Liberia, the West African region, and continental Africa.

As a child rights, specialty educational, cultural heritage, tourism, and holistic healthcare advocate and promoter, TJLF partners with related humanitarian development organizations to create combined child rights, education, empowerment, healthcare, and African cultural tourism programs for development across Liberia.

OUR STRATEGIC MISSION

The strategic mission of Joshua Legacy Foundation is to:

1. Develop Education Model

Create an effective, result-oriented, manageable, and sustainable "education development model" that transforms at-risk, orphaned, underprivileged, and vulnerable youth into spirit-filled future leaders.

2. Provide Enhanced Education

Provide comprehensive educational resources (uniforms, stationery, fees, shoes, textbooks, materials, feeding, accommodation, toiletries) throughout kindergarten through university education.

3. Leadership Development

Deliver Leadership Development and Democracy & Governance programs teaching youth to evaluate and elect representatives and form community governing structures.

4. Healthcare Services

Establish comprehensive, cutting-edge modern medical and healthcare facilities catering to students and communities with wellness programs ensuring healthy, wholesome functioning populations.

5. Sustainable Infrastructure

Plan, design, construct, and operate sustainable green environments to house institutions, academies, and community centers showcasing program efficacy.

6. Teach Kingdom Principles

Teach participants "Spirit-filled Democracy, Humanistic Capitalism, Godly Governance," and develop "Kingdom Leadership Skills" rooted in faith-based philosophy.
